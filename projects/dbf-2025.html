01 · Mission Brief

The Competition Challenge

The 2025 AIAA Design/Build/Fly competition required teams to design, build, and fly a remote-piloted aircraft capable of two distinct mission elements: carrying auxiliary fuel tanks as an external payload, and deploying a GPS-guided glider mid-flight to a specified target.

As Team Leader, I was responsible for the overall design direction, technical decision-making, build coordination, and team management from concept through to fly-off in Tucson.

FIG 01 "Akashtori 2.0" airborne at the Tucson competition site, Arizona — the high-wing, dual-motor carrier aircraft built by Team Meghdoot for the AIAA DBF X-1 Supersonic Flight Test mission.
02 · Leadership Role

Leading the MIST Team

As Team Leader, my responsibilities went well beyond engineering contributions — they encompassed the full project life-cycle from concept through to international fly-off:

  • Conceptual design lead — defining the carrier-glider configuration and core trade studies.
  • Build coordination — splitting work across sub-teams (aerodynamics, structures, propulsion, payload, avionics).
  • Schedule & milestone management — keeping design reviews, fabrication, and flight testing on the competition timeline.
  • Stakeholder management — liaising with department leadership, sponsors, and the AIAA committee.
  • Travel & competition logistics — coordinating the team's deployment to Tucson, including aircraft transport and on-site preparation.
03 · Key Engineering Element

The GPS-Guided Glider System

The defining technical challenge of the 2025 mission was the GPS-guided glider — a self-contained sub-vehicle carried internally by the main aircraft, deployed mid-flight, and required to autonomously navigate to a designated target.

Designing this required tight coupling between the carrier aircraft and the glider: a payload bay with reliable release mechanism, a glider with sufficient lift-to-drag for the deployment altitude, an onboard GPS-guidance system, and integration that didn't compromise either platform's flight characteristics.
