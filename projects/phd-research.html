PhD Research — Robust Multimodal Perception for Edge UAVs

Doctoral research at the Deakin Institute for Intelligent Systems Research and Innovation (IISRI) developing lightweight, edge-deployable multimodal (RGB–thermal) fusion for real-time UAV perception that remains accurate when sensor streams are absent or degraded. Research is ongoing — outputs will include multiple conference papers and journal articles. One early conference paper (FGRF, ICONIP 2026) has been produced so far; more work is in progress.

01 · Research Vision

Perception That Survives Sensor Failure

Aerial robots increasingly rely on multiple complementary sensors — visible (RGB) cameras for texture and colour, thermal cameras for heat signatures that cut through smoke and darkness. Fusing them yields far better perception than either alone. But real flights are messy: a lens fogs, a thermal core saturates, a cable drops a frame, glare washes out an image. The moment a modality degrades or disappears, naïve fusion models that assume every input is always present can fail — exactly when reliability matters most.

My thesis tackles this head-on: building fusion architectures that are explicitly aware of modality reliability, gracefully re-weighting or falling back to the surviving sensor stream rather than collapsing. The constraint that shapes every design decision is that this must run on-board, in real time, on a power- and compute-limited UAV — not in a data centre.

02 · First-Author Output

First Conference Output: FGRF (ICONIP 2026)

The first paper from this doctoral work is FGRF — a compact RGB–thermal classifier for UAV wildfire detection, designed to keep working under modality failure. A learned gating mechanism modulates each modality's contribution, so when one stream degrades or disappears, the network re-weights to what remains rather than failing. The model is kept deliberately compact for edge deployment on UAV-class hardware.

FGRF is an early first-author output; the broader PhD programme will produce additional journal and conference contributions extending this work further.

03 · Approach

Built for the Edge

Robustness is only useful if it fits on the aircraft. The work pairs reliability-aware fusion with an aggressive efficiency budget — model compression and deployment-oriented optimisation (TensorRT) targeting NVIDIA Jetson-class compute — so that inference happens on-board within the UAV's real-time and power constraints.

04 · Research Thrusts

Research Directions

🔀
Reliability-Aware Fusion
Architectures that estimate per-modality reliability online and gate fusion accordingly, degrading gracefully instead of catastrophically.
🪶
Lightweight Models
Compression, distillation and deployment-aware design to fit accurate perception within UAV compute and power budgets.
🔥
Aerial Wildfire Perception
Wildfire detection and classification as a demanding real-world testbed where smoke, heat and glare routinely degrade sensors.
🛰️
On-Board Decision-Making
Moving inference and decisions on-board for autonomous operation without reliance on a continuous ground link.
