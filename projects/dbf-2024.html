01 · Mission Brief

The Competition Challenge

The 2024 AIAA DBF mission set required teams to design a fixed-wing UAV that could swap between distinct payload configurations and meet a strict storage-volume requirement — driving the foldable-wing solution.

This was my first AIAA DBF competition, joining the MIST team as a member contributing to fabrication and flight testing. The lessons learned here directly shaped my approach as Team Leader the following year.

FIG 01 "Akashtori" — Team Novonil's MIST DBF 2024 aircraft at Wichita, Kansas, USA. A high-wing composite design with a nose-mounted tractor propeller, conventional tail and foldable wings.
02 · My Contributions

As a Team Member

As a member of the MIST DBF 2024 team, my contributions focused on the hands-on execution side of the project:

  • Fabrication — composite layups, structural assembly, and airframe build.
  • Flight testing — pre-competition test flights and tuning.
  • Competition deployment — on-site work in Wichita including assembly, scrutineering, and mission flight preparation.
03 · Engineering Highlights

Foldable Wings & Modular Payload

Two engineering elements defined the 2024 aircraft. The foldable-wing design allowed the aircraft to fit within the competition's specified storage envelope while still providing the wing area needed for stable flight. The modular payload bay enabled rapid swap between different payload configurations between mission flights.

FIG 02 The folded-wing configuration — wings stow upward in a V to meet the competition's storage volume constraint, then deploy to full span for flight.
FIG 03 Pre-flight checks at the Wichita competition site — final systems verification before technical inspection and the official mission flights.

06 · Why It Mattered

From Member to Leader

The 2024 competition was foundational.
